The order of taking the tiles is East, South, West, and North (counterclockwise). Each player takes 2 stacks of tiles for 4 times, 4 tiles each time, 16 tiles in total.

The direction of taking the tiles is clockwise, from the right to the left (â†). After everyone gets a hand of 16 tiles, East takes 1 more tile.

If the hand includes Seasons or Flowers, all of them are placed face-up in front of the playersâ€™ hand and replaced by the tiles from the end of the wall. The order is also East, South, West, and North.

The direction of replacing tiles is from the left to the right (â†’). If the replaced tile is also one of Seasons or Flowers, do the same thing mentioned above after each player does the replacement.

C. Playing

The game starts by East discarding 1 tile from the hand to the middle of the wall.

Each player takes 1 tile from the beginning of the wall to the hand, and then discards 1 tile from the hand to the middle.

Again, the order of taking and discarding 1 tile is East, South, West, and North.

In order to win the game, each player needs to gather 6 sets of tiles as soon as possible.

The hand must consist of 1 set of Pair and any combination of 5 sets of Kong, Pung, or Chow.

There are 4 kinds of sets: Kong, Pung, Chow, and Pair. The following table displays all of them in detail.
