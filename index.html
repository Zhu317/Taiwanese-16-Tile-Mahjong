B. Seats and Deals

There are 4 seats in total: East (東), South (南), West (西), and North (北). The player is at East’s seat.

In front of each seat is a row of tiles, 18-tile long and 2-tile high, 36 tiles in total. The 4 rows of tiles form the 144-tile wall.

The deal starts by East throwing 3 dice. The sum of dice decides where to start taking the tiles and where the end and the beginning of the wall are. The following table illustrates further details.

The order of taking the tiles is East, South, West, and North (counterclockwise). Each player takes 2 stacks of tiles for 4 times, 4 tiles each time, 16 tiles in total.

The direction of taking the tiles is clockwise, from the right to the left (←). After everyone gets a hand of 16 tiles, East takes 1 more tile.

If the hand includes Seasons or Flowers, all of them are placed face-up in front of the players’ hand and replaced by the tiles from the end of the wall. The order is also East, South, West, and North.

The direction of replacing tiles is from the left to the right (→). If the replaced tile is also one of Seasons or Flowers, do the same thing mentioned above after each player does the replacement.

C. Playing

The game starts by East discarding 1 tile from the hand to the middle of the wall.

Each player takes 1 tile from the beginning of the wall to the hand, and then discards 1 tile from the hand to the middle.

Again, the order of taking and discarding 1 tile is East, South, West, and North.

In order to win the game, each player needs to gather 6 sets of tiles as soon as possible.

The hand must consist of 1 set of Pair and any combination of 5 sets of Kong, Pung, or Chow.

There are 4 kinds of sets: Kong, Pung, Chow, and Pair. The following table displays all of them in detail.

Sets Definition Examples Rules with Scenarios
Kong A set of 4 identical tiles 🀀🀀🀀🀀
Scenario 1: Suppose East has a set of identical tiles “🀀🀀🀀” in the hand.
  1. When South, West, or North discards the tile “🀀” to the middle, East can claim “Kong”.
  2. After claiming, East takes the discarded tile “🀀” to meld with its 3 identical tiles “🀀🀀🀀”, and then places them face-up in front of his/her hand.
  3. After that, East takes 1 tile from the end of the wall to the hand, and then discards 1 tile from the hand to the middle.
  4. After the tile discarded, if no one claims “Kong”, “Pung”, or “Chow”, the next player of East, South, continues the game.
  5. The other players (South, West, North) do the same thing as East when they can claim “Kong”.
Scenario 2: Suppose East has a set of identical tiles “🀀🀀🀀” in the hand.
  1. When East takes 1 tile from the beginning of the wall and the taken tile is “🀀”, he/she can claim “Kong”.
  2. After claiming, East melds the taken tile “🀀” with its 3 identical tiles “🀀🀀🀀”, and then places them face-down in front of his/her hand.
  3. After that, East takes 1 tile from the end of the wall to the hand, and then discards 1 tile from the hand to the middle.
  4. After the tile discarded, if no one claims “Kong”, “Pung”, or “Chow”, the next player of East, South, continues the game.
  5. The other players (South, West, North) do the same thing as East when they can claim “Kong”.
Scenario 3: Suppose East has a set of identical tiles “🀀🀀🀀🀀” in the hand right after 16 tiles dealt to every player.
  1. After East takes 1 more tile from the beginning of the wall and each player’s Seasons or Flowers are replaced, East can claim “Kong”.
  2. After claiming, East places the set of tiles “🀀🀀🀀🀀” face-down in front of his/her hand.
  3. After that, East takes 1 tile from the end of the wall to the hand, and then discards 1 tile from the hand to the middle.
  4. After the tile discarded, if no one claims “Kong”, “Pung”, or “Chow”, the next player of East, South, continues the game.
  5. The other players (South, West, North) do the same thing as East when they can claim “Kong”. (Except for the replacement of Seasons or Flowers because this happens before game starts.)
Scenario 4: Suppose East has already had a set of Pung “🀀🀀🀀” placed face-up in front of his/her hand.
  1. When East takes 1 tile from the beginning of the wall and the taken tile is “🀀”, he/she can claim “Kong”.
  2. After claiming, East places the taken tile “🀀” face-up next to the Pung “🀀🀀🀀” in front of his/her hand.
  3. After that, East takes 1 tile from the end of the wall to the hand, and then discards 1 tile from the hand to the middle.
  4. After the tile discarded, if no one claims “Kong”, “Pung”, or “Chow”, the next player of East, South, continues the game.
  5. The other players (South, West, North) do the same thing as East when they can claim “Kong”.
Pung A set of 3 identical tiles 🀙🀙🀙
Scenario: Suppose East has a set of identical tiles “🀙🀙” in the hand.
  1. When South, West, or North discards the tile “🀙” to the middle, East can claim “Pung”.
  2. After claiming, East takes the discarded tile “🀙” to meld with its 2 identical tiles “🀙🀙”, and then places them face-up in front of his/her hand.
  3. After that, East discards 1 tile from the hand to the middle.
  4. After the tile discarded, if no one claims “Kong”, “Pung”, or “Chow”, the next player of East, South, continues the game.
  5. The other players (South, West, North) do the same thing as East when they can claim “Pung”.
Chow A set of 3 sequential tiles 🀇🀈🀉
Scenario 1: Suppose East has a set of tiles “🀇🀉” in the hand.
  1. When the previous player, North, discards the tile “🀈” to the middle, East can claim “Chow”.
  2. After claiming, East takes the discarded tile “🀈” to put it between the 2 tiles “🀇🀉”, and then places them face-up in front of his/her hand.
  3. After that, East discards 1 tile from the hand to the middle.
  4. After the tile discarded, if no one claims “Kong”, “Pung”, or “Chow”, the next player of East, South, continues the game.
  5. The other players (South, West, North) do the same thing as East when they can claim “Chow”.
Scenario 2: Suppose East has a set of tiles “🀇🀉” in the hand, and South has set of identical tiles “🀈🀈🀈” in the hand.
  1. When the previous player of East, North, discards the tile “🀈” to the middle, East can claim “Chow” and South can claim “Kong” .
  2. Because both players want the same tile “🀈” at the same time, and the precedence of Kong is higher than that of Chow, in this case, South can take the discarded tile “🀈”, while East can’t.
  3. The same rules apply to other players (South, West, North) if they face the similar scenarios.
Scenario 3: Suppose East has a set of tiles “🀇🀉” in the hand, and West has set of identical tiles “🀈🀈” in the hand.
  1. When the previous player of East, North, discards the tile “🀈” to the middle, East can claim “Chow” and West can claim “Pung”.
  2. Because both players want the same tile “🀈” at the same time, and the precedence of Pung is higher than that of Chow, in this case, West can take the discarded tile “🀈”, while East can’t.
  3. The same rules apply to other players (South, West, North) if they face the similar scenarios.
Pair A set of 2 identical tiles 🀐🀐 There is no way to claim “Pair” under any circumstances.

The melded Kong, Pung, and Chow placed face-up (or face-down for Kong) in front of the players’ hands cannot be modified until the end of the game. The only possible modification is mentioned in the Scenario 4 of Kong.

If one or more sets of Pung/Chow are already in the players’ hand after 16 tiles dealt to every player, or they can be formed after a tile taken from the beginning of the wall, there is no need to claim “Pung/Chow”, and they can be modified at will.

If the taken tile is one of Seasons or Flowers, do the replacement mentioned above until getting a regular tile.

D. Mahjong and Draw

After any player gathers 1 set of Pair and any combination of 5 sets of Kong, Pung, or Chow, he/she can simply claim “Mahjong” to win the game.

A player can claim “Mahjong” by taking 1 tile from the beginning or end of the wall to the hand, or by the tile discarded by others.

The tile can be the missing tile of a set of Pung, a set of Chow, even a set of Pair.

E. How to Play?

Check the following table to know how to play this simple Mahjong game.

Buttons/Selector/Tiles Functions
Start Game
  • Press to start or restart the game.
  • It is disabled when the game is in progress.
  • It can be pressed after some player claims “Mahjong” or the game is a draw.
Chow
  • When the player (East) can claim “Chow”, there will be some options to choose.
  • The options will be removed after choosing.
Pung
  • Press it when the player (East) can claim “Pung”.
  • It is disabled after the player presses or the game finishes (some player claims “Mahjong” or the game is a draw).
Kong
  • Press it when the player (East) can claim “Kong”.
  • It is disabled after the player presses or the game finishes (some player claims “Mahjong” or the game is a draw).
Pass
  • Press it when the player (East) can claim “Chow”, “Pung”, or “Kong” but decides not to.
  • It is disabled after the player presses or the game finishes (some player claims “Mahjong” or the game is a draw).
Tiles
  • Press any tile, both the hand and the taken tile, of the player (East) to discard it to the middle.
  • They are disabled when the player (East) can claim “Chow”, “Pung”, or “Kong” and has not decided yet.
  • They are also disabled after the game finishes (some player claims “Mahjong” or the game is a draw).

If you are ready, just press “Start Game”.
